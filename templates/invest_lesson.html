What is Investing?

Lesson 1 of 4 · ~5 min read

💰 Saving vs Investing

Saving means putting money aside for future use — usually in a bank account or FD. Investing means putting money to work so it grows faster than inflation.

₹10,000 in a savings account at 3.5% for 20 years = ₹19,898
₹10,000 in an index mutual fund at 12% for 20 years = ₹96,462 🚀

📈 The Power of Compounding

Compounding means earning returns on your returns. Albert Einstein reportedly called it the "8th wonder of the world."

⏰
Start at 20

₹2,000/month for 40 years = ₹1.17 crore

⌛
Start at 30

₹2,000/month for 30 years = ₹35 lakhs

🐢
Start at 40

₹2,000/month for 20 years = ₹9.7 lakhs

Assumed 12% annual return (Nifty 50 historical average). The 10-year difference between starting at 20 vs 30 gives you 3× more wealth!

⏰ Time is Your Biggest Asset

You don't need a lot of money to start — you need time. Even ₹500/month invested consistently from age 22 will outperform ₹5,000/month started at age 35.

💡 Key Takeaway: The best time to start investing was yesterday. The second best time is today.

Types of Investments

Lesson 2 of 4 · ~7 min read

📊 Stocks High Risk

A stock is a slice of ownership in a company. When the company profits, your stock value rises. You can also earn dividends.

📈 Return10–35% p.a.
⏱️ Horizon5+ years
💰 Min₹1 (1 share)

Best for: Long-term wealth creation. Avoid if you panic-sell during dips!

🌐 Mutual Funds Medium Risk

A mutual fund pools money from many investors. A professional fund manager invests it across stocks, bonds, or both.

📈 Return8–18% p.a.
⏱️ Horizon3–7 years
💰 Min₹500/month SIP

Best for: Beginners who want diversification without stock-picking.

🔁 SIP Medium Risk

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is not an asset but a method — invest a fixed amount in mutual funds every month automatically.

📈 Return12–15% avg
⏱️ Horizon5+ years
💰 Min₹100/month

Superpower: Rupee Cost Averaging — you buy more units when prices fall, less when they rise.

🏛️ Bonds Low Risk

A bond is a loan you give to a government or company. They pay you fixed interest (coupon) and return principal at maturity.

📈 Return6–8% p.a.
⏱️ Horizon1–10 years
💰 Min₹1,000

Government bonds (G-Secs) are safest. Corporate bonds offer higher returns but more risk.

🏦 Fixed Deposit Very Low Risk

An FD locks your money with a bank for a fixed duration at a guaranteed interest rate. Principal is fully protected.

📈 Return5.5–7.5% p.a.
⏱️ Horizon7 days – 10 yrs
💰 Min₹1,000

FDs barely beat inflation (5–6%). Use them for your emergency fund, not long-term wealth.

💡 Golden Rule of Diversification: Never put all your eggs in one basket. A healthy portfolio mixes different asset classes based on your age and risk appetite.

Risk vs Return

Lesson 3 of 4 · ~6 min read

⚖️ The Fundamental Tradeoff

In finance, higher potential return always comes with higher risk. There is no such thing as high return with zero risk — anyone promising that is running a scam.

6–7%
Very Low Risk
🏛️ Bonds
6–8%
Low Risk
🌐 MF/SIP
10–15%
Medium Risk
📊 Stocks
12–35%
High Risk
🪙 Crypto
-80 to +200%
Very High Risk

🎲 Types of Risk

📉
Market Risk

Overall market crash (2008, 2020). All stocks fall together.

🏢
Company Risk

A specific company fails (e.g. Yes Bank 2020). Diversification helps.

💸
Inflation Risk

Returns that don't beat inflation lose real value over time.

🛡️ How to Manage Risk

  • Diversify: Spread investments across asset classes, sectors, and geographies.
  • Long time horizon: Market volatility smooths out over 7–10 years.
  • Don't panic sell: Selling during a crash locks in your losses permanently.
  • Age rule of thumb: Your equity % ≈ 100 minus your age. At 25: 75% equity, 25% debt.
✅ Risk ≠ Bad: Controlled risk, taken with a long horizon, is how wealth is built. The real risk for young investors is being too conservative.

Beginner Investment Strategy

Lesson 4 of 4 · ~6 min read

🪜 The 5-Step Beginner Roadmap

1
Build Your Emergency Fund First

Before any investment, save 3–6 months of expenses in a high-yield savings account or liquid mutual fund. This is your financial safety net.

Goal: ₹75,000–₹1.5 lakh (for ₹25k/month spender)
2
Clear High-Interest Debt

Credit card debt at 36%/year? Paying that off is a guaranteed 36% return — better than any investment.

3
Start a SIP in an Index Fund

Open a mutual fund account (Zerodha Coin, Groww, or Kuvera). Start a monthly SIP in a Nifty 50 or Nifty 500 index fund. Even ₹500/month is perfect to begin.

💡 Index funds beat 85% of actively managed funds over 10+ years, with lower fees.
4
Automate & Increase Gradually

Set the SIP on auto-debit on salary day. Every year, increase your SIP by 10–15% to match income growth (called "step-up SIP").

5
Review Annually, Not Daily

Check your portfolio once a year, not daily. Obsessing over daily NAV fluctuations leads to emotional decisions and panic selling — the #1 wealth destroyer.

📊 Suggested Portfolio for a 22-Year-Old

70% Equity MF/SIP
15% Bonds
10% Gold
5% FD

As you age, gradually shift allocation from equity to debt for stability.
